5000 Years of Civilisation

Culture & Heritage

Art, dance, music, festivals and ancient wisdom — India's culture is humanity's living heritage.

Celebrations

Festivals of India

India's festival calendar is the world's most vibrant — there's always something to celebrate.

🎨

Holi

March

The Festival of Colours erupts across India — strangers become friends, streets become canvases, and joy is the only religion. Best celebrated in Mathura, Vrindavan, and Jaipur.

🪔

Diwali

Oct – Nov

The Festival of Lights illuminates the entire country for five nights. Millions of clay diyas, fireworks, sweets and family reunions mark the triumph of light over darkness.

💃

Navratri

Sep – Oct

Nine nights of music, dance, and devotion. In Gujarat, the Garba dance fills stadiums. In West Bengal, Durga Puja turns cities into open-air art galleries.

🐪

Pushkar Camel Fair

Nov

The world's largest camel fair transforms the Rajasthani desert into a carnival of colour, culture, and commerce — 50,000 camels, folk music, and the sacred Pushkar Lake.

🌾

Pongal / Makar Sankranti

January

The harvest festival celebrated across India under different names — kite festivals fill skies in Ahmedabad, while Tamil Nadu prepares sweet pongal rice and honors cattle.

🌙

Eid & Christmas

Varies

India's secular fabric means Eid is celebrated with biryani feasts and charity, while Christmas in Goa and Kerala is a magical mix of midnight mass, carols and fireworks.

Built Legacy

Architectural Wonders

From cave temples to Mughal mausoleums — India's stone speaks through millennia.

🕌

Mughal Architecture

Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Fatehpur Sikri — the Mughals left a legacy of sublime marble and sandstone grandeur.

🛕

Dravidian Temples

Towering gopurams with thousands of sculpted deities — Madurai, Thanjavur and Mahabalipuram astound.

🏯

Rajput Forts

Amber, Mehrangarh, Chittorgarh — the warrior Rajputs built citadels that dominate the skyline for centuries.

🪨

Rock-Cut Caves

Ajanta, Ellora, and Elephanta — ancient monks carved entire temple complexes into solid rock over centuries.
