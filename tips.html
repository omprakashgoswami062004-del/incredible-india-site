Essential Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before you embark on your Indian adventure.

At a Glance

India Essentials

🌡️

Climate & Best Time

Oct – Mar Best for most regions. Cool, dry, ideal for sightseeing.
Apr – Jun Hot & dry in plains. Ideal for hill stations.
Jun – Sep Monsoon. Lush & green but heavy rain. Avoid coastal travel.
💳

Money & Budget

Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ₹1 ≈ $0.012 USD
Budget: ₹1,500–2,500/day backpacker; ₹5,000–10,000/day mid-range
UPI & Cash: Digital payments widespread. Carry cash for rural areas.
📋

Visa Requirements

e-Visa: Available for 170+ countries. Apply online 4+ days before arrival.
Validity: 30-day, 1-year, or 5-year tourist visas available.
Apply at: indianvisaonline.gov.in
🏥

Health & Safety

Vaccinations: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended.
Water: Only drink bottled or filtered water. Avoid ice in budget places.
Insurance: Comprehensive travel insurance is essential.

Getting Around

Transport in India

🚆

Indian Railways

The world's 4th largest rail network. Book on IRCTC app or website. Classes range from AC First Class to unreserved. Book 90 days in advance for popular routes.

irctc.co.in →
✈️

Domestic Flights

IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet connect all major cities. Book 3–6 weeks early for best prices. Delhi–Mumbai can be as low as ₹1,500.

State buses are cheapest; private sleeper coaches are comfortable for overnight journeys. redBus app is excellent for booking across India.

Respect & Etiquette

Cultural Do's & Don'ts

✅ Do's

  • Remove shoes before entering temples, mosques, and many homes
  • Dress modestly at religious sites — cover shoulders and knees
  • Use your right hand for eating, giving, and receiving
  • Greet with "Namaste" (palms together) — it's always appreciated
  • Bargain politely in markets — it's expected and fun
  • Carry small change for auto-rickshaws and local vendors
  • Ask permission before photographing people

❌ Don'ts

  • Don't point feet toward religious icons or people
  • Avoid public displays of affection in conservative areas
  • Don't eat beef in Hindu-majority areas or pork in mosques
  • Never touch someone's head — it's considered sacred
  • Don't photograph military installations or airports
  • Avoid drinking tap water directly anywhere in India
  • Don't disrespect any religion — India is deeply multi-faith

Pack Smart

India Packing List

👕 Clothing
  • Lightweight breathable fabrics (cotton/linen)
  • Modest outfits (covered shoulders & knees)
  • Sandals + comfortable walking shoes
  • Light scarf/shawl (temple cover-up)
  • Rain jacket if visiting during monsoon
💊 Health
  • Oral rehydration salts (ORS)
  • Anti-diarrheal medication
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Mosquito repellent (DEET-based)
  • Basic first-aid kit & prescription meds
📱 Tech & Documents
  • Indian SIM card (Airtel/Jio at airport)
  • Universal power adapter
  • Printed & digital visa copies
  • Offline maps (Maps.me)
  • Translation app (Google Translate)
🎒 Essentials
  • Water bottle with filter (LifeStraw)
  • Padlock for hostel lockers
  • Wet wipes & hand sanitizer
  • Small backpack for day trips
  • Cash in rupees (ATMs available everywhere)
